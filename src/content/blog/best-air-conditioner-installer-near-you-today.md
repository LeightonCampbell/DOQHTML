## Look for NATE-Certified Technicians

When searching for an installer, look for the NATE (North American Technician Excellence) seal. This certification ensures the technician has the specialized knowledge to handle modern, high-efficiency systems.

Proper installation is actually more important than the brand of the unit itself. A poorly installed "top-tier" unit will run inefficiently and break down years before it should.

## Compare Load Calculations, Not Just Prices

A "best" installer won't just look at your old unit and swap it for the same size. They should perform a Manual J Load Calculation to ensure the system is perfectly sized for your home's current insulation and square footage.

Ask for written estimates from at least three different companies. This allows you to compare warranties, labor guarantees, and financing options side-by-side.
