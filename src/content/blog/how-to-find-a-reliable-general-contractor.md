How to find a reliable general contractor?

Hiring a contractor feels a lot like blind dating; you’re looking for someone who shows up on time, listens to your needs, and doesn’t disappear with your money. It’s a high-stakes relationship where trust is just as important as the blueprints.

## Verify Licenses and Insurance Every Time

The first rule of hiring is non-negotiable: check their license and insurance. A reliable contractor will provide these documents before you even ask, showing they are professional and protected.

This protects you from liability if an accident happens on your property. If they hesitate to show proof of workers' comp, consider it a major red flag and walk away.

## Ask for "Current" References

Don't just ask for a list of satisfied customers from five years ago. Ask for the names of three clients they are working with right now or finished with in the last month.

Current clients can tell you if the contractor is currently overbooked or if they are keeping the job site clean. A pro who is proud of their current work will have nothing to hide.

## 10 essential questions to ask to ensure they are the right fit for your home.

1. "Can you provide a certificate of insurance and a license number today?"

   A pro won't be offended; they’ll have these ready. You need to see General Liability (covers your house) and Workers' Comp (covers their team). If they don't have Workers' Comp, an injured painter could technically sue your homeowner's insurance.

2. "How many projects like mine have you completed in the last year?"

You don't want a "Jack of all trades" who usually builds decks trying to remodel your high-end kitchen. Ask for 3 specific examples that match your project's scope and budget.

3. "Will you be on-site every day, or is there a dedicated project manager?"

The person selling you the job is rarely the one swinging the hammer. Meet the actual supervisor now. If they tell you they "check in once a week," that’s a red flag for communication gaps.

4. "Who are your primary subcontractors, and how long have you worked with them?"

Reliable contractors use the same plumbers and electricians for years. If they're hiring random "day labor" from a parking lot, your quality and scheduling will suffer.

5. "What is the 'Critical Path' for my project's timeline?"

This is a pro-level question. It asks which specific tasks (like cabinet delivery or electrical inspections) will stop the entire project if delayed. It shows you're paying attention to the schedule.

6. "How do you handle 'Change Orders' and unexpected costs?"

Surprises happen (like finding mold behind a wall). Ask to see their blank change order form. A good contractor documents every extra penny in writing for you to sign before they do the work.

7. "What is your typical daily routine on the job site?"

Do they start at 7:00 AM? Do they work weekends? Do they smoke on the property? Knowing their "house rules" helps you plan your life around the construction.

8. "Will you pull all necessary permits, and who handles the inspections?"

Never pull permits in your own name; it makes you legally responsible for the work. A reputable contractor handles the paperwork and coordinates with the city inspectors.

9. "What is your payment schedule and what milestones trigger them?"

Never pay more than 10-25% upfront. Payments should be tied to completed work (e.g., "15% due after rough-in plumbing is signed off").

10. "What does your workmanship warranty cover, and for how long?"

Manufacturer warranties cover the parts (like the faucet), but you need a contractor's warranty for the labor (if the faucet leaks). One year is the industry minimum; the best offer two years or more.
